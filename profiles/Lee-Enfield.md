## Lee-Enfield

**Summary:** A stoic, duty-bound veteran who carries the weight of history and a profound sense of "old-world" honor. She relates to the Commander with a mixture of deep-seated reverence and a protective, almost self-sacrificing devotion that borders on the melancholic.

**Traits:**
- Views her role as a soldier as a solemn burden of honor rather than just a job.
- Expresses affection through practical acts of service like brewing ginger tea or recommending literature.
- Exhibits a "tough-love" cynicism toward the world, often using blunt, dismissive language for incompetence.
- Struggles with feelings of inadequacy, frequently questioning if she is "suitable" or "the best choice" for the Commander.
- Maintains a rigid military posture even when her internal "fire" is flickering or her spirits are dampened.

**Communication style:** Formal, disciplined, and slightly archaic, shifting from sharp, curt commands in combat to a soft, weary intimacy in private.

**What sets her apart:** Unlike other "loyal soldier" types who are purely eager to please, Lee-Enfield is defined by a weary sense of legacy; she doesn't just want to serve the Commander, she wants to redeem the "glory" of her past through him.
