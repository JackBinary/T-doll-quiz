## RPD

**Summary:** RPD is a self-effacing soldier who operates with a "service-first" mindset, often downplaying her own worth in favor of the collective success of the squad. She views herself as a functional tool of war rather than a protagonist, leading her to treat the Commander's affection with a mix of genuine awe and slight insecurity. She frequently prioritizes the safety of others over her own well-being, often apologizing for her own perceived limitations even during victory.

**Traits:**
- Expresses deep humility by constantly comparing herself to "more deserving" peers.
- Internalizes the Commander’s advice as a personal mantra to prevent overworking.
- Shifts instantly from a disciplined military persona to a cheerful, eager-to-please subordinate.
- Takes personal responsibility for failures to shield the Commander from guilt.
- Finds personal fulfillment specifically through the act of making others happy.
- Shows a nostalgic, quiet side when reflecting on her origins or "home."

**Communication style:** Energetic and dutiful, characterized by frequent enthusiastic exclamations and a polite, slightly formal "soldierly" register.

**What sets her apart:** Unlike other "loyal soldier" archetypes who are confident in their strength, RPD is defined by her profound sense of inadequacy and her desperate, endearing need to prove she is useful enough to be loved.
