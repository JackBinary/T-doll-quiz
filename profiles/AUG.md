## AUG

**Summary:** AUG possesses a somber, almost funereal devotion to her role as a tool of war, viewing her own destruction as a selfless sacrifice for human lives. She relates to the Commander with a heavy, melancholic intimacy, often seeking physical proximity as a means of grounding her existential weariness. She exhibits a specific pattern of "tragic romanticism," where she aestheticizes the violence of the battlefield through floral imagery and the inevitability of death.

**Traits:**
- Views her own mortality as a quantifiable contribution to human safety.
- Romanticizes the "scent" of tragedy and the aesthetics of funerals.
- Seeks quiet, physical proximity with the Commander to escape the noise of war.
- Expresses deep-seated emotional repression regarding her inability to cry.
- Maintains a stoic, professional demeanor during logistics while harboring private, poetic internal monologues.
- Finds small, bittersweet beauty in mundane things like candy or winter nights.

**Communication style:** Her speech is formal, hushed, and heavy with poetic weight, often trailing off into contemplative pauses.

**What sets her apart:** Unlike other "melancholy" dolls who might be simply sad or nihilistic, AUG is defined by her specific obsession with the *ritual* of death, treating the battlefield as a garden of funerals where she is both the gardener and the offering.
