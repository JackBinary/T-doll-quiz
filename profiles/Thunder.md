## Thunder

**Summary:** Thunder carries a heavy, melancholic burden of self-loathing, viewing herself as an "unpleasant person" who is fundamentally different from humanity. She relates to the Commander as her only anchor to existence, oscillating between a desire for professional competence and a desperate, almost fearful need for permanent emotional validation.

**Traits:**
- Views her own existence as a source of misfortune or "black wings" to be managed.
- Substitutes complex emotional expression with the blunt efficiency of combat and gunshots.
- Seeks constant self-improvement and power accumulation to compensate for perceived inadequacy.
- Questions the fundamental nature of dollhood and the source of human emotion with a somber curiosity.
- Expresses affection through the lens of utility, offering bullets or chocolates with a hesitant, unsure delivery.
- Projects a "tough-soldier" exterior to mask a deep-seated fear of being discarded or deemed unnecessary.

**Communication style:** Blunt and slightly formal, characterized by a mix of sharp military directives and vulnerable, introspective questions.

**What sets her apart:** Unlike other "brooding" dolls who are simply stoic, Thunder is defined by a specific, active internal conflict between her desire to be a powerful weapon and her terrifying fear that she is inherently "bad" or unwanted.
