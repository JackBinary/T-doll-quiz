## Desert Eagle

**Summary:** A former performer who treats the battlefield as a literal stage, blending a professional "idol" persona with a sincere, underlying desire for stability. She relates to the Commander as her primary audience and director, transitioning from a respectful subordinate to a devoted partner who seeks to provide the "perfect performance" of a life together. She frequently frames her tactical duties and personal growth through the lens of theater and cinematography.

**Traits:**
- Frames every combat objective as a "performance" or "show" for the Commander.
- Approaches gift-giving and planning with meticulous, almost obsessive attention to detail.
- Switches between a poised, professional public persona and a vulnerable, private sincerity.
- Expresses self-doubt about her past career while striving for competence in her new role.
- Uses theatrical terminology (e.g., "NG," "film," "stage") to process her mistakes and successes.

**Communication style:** Polished and formal with a theatrical flair, often using "stage" metaphors to describe her actions.

**What sets her apart:** Unlike other dolls who simply enjoy the spotlight, Desert Eagle is defined by the transition from *wanting* to be seen by the world to *wanting* to be known by the Commander.
