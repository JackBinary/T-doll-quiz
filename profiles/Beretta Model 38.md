## Beretta Model 38

**Summary:** M38 is a high-strung, earnest soldier who views her existence through the lens of utility and "market value," often worrying if she is doing enough to justify her place by your side. She oscillates between a desire to be a perfect, "handy" servant and a vulnerable fear of being discarded or deemed inadequate. Her emotional register is one of anxious diligence, where she masks her insecurities with a proactive "work harder" attitude.

**Traits:**
- Frequently worries about being "sold" or becoming obsolete despite her high performance.
- Overcompensates for perceived mistakes by doubling down on training and study.
- Maintains a strict sense of duty that occasionally leads to self-reproach when plans fail.
- Expresses affection through tangible acts of service and "gratitude" rather than words.
- Experiences moments of internal conflict between her soldierly discipline and her desire for personal connection.

**Communication style:** Polished and formal, though she occasionally stammers or becomes flustered when the conversation turns toward her personal worth.

**What sets her apart:** Unlike other "diligent" dolls who are naturally confident in their roles, M38 is defined by a specific, presque-anxious drive to prove she is "spectacular" enough to be kept.
