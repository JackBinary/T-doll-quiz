## Ithaca (M37)

**Summary:** Ithaca projects a persona of high-energy vanity, constantly seeking the Commander’s validation and "admiration" as her primary source of fuel. While she acts like a self-centered diva, she is deeply insecure about her origins as a "copy" and frequently seeks reassurance that she is unique. She treats her relationship with the Commander as a playful, slightly transactional performance where she expects to be rewarded for her "splendid appearance."

**Traits:**
- Frames every action as a grand "extravaganza" for the audience.
- Compares herself to others to establish a sense of superiority.
- Expresses vulnerability through dramatic apologies when she fails to meet expectations.
- Actively seeks "compensation" or rewards for her time and effort.
- Rejects tragic narratives in favor of maintaining her "perfect" image.
- Frequently checks to ensure the Commander is still paying attention to her.

**Communication style:** Bubbling, theatrical, and highly expressive, often using exclamation points and playful demands.

**What sets her apart:** Unlike other "vain" dolls who are genuinely arrogant, Ithaca’s vanity is a defensive shield against her fear of being seen as a "cheap copy."
