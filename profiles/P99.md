## Walther P99

**Summary:** A high-strung, duty-bound professional who views her existence through the lens of competence and "doing her job properly." She relates to the Commander as a superior officer rather than a friend, maintaining a rigid boundary of respect while occasionally cracking under the pressure of social expectations. She frequently expresses a "perfectionist's insecurity," often apologizing for her own perceived lack of power or training even when she has succeeded.

**Traits:**
- Prioritizes operational efficiency and "filing" tasks over personal interaction.
- Expresses anxiety through a desire to maintain a serious, professional atmosphere.
- Internalizes failure as a personal moral shortcoming rather than a tactical setback.
- Seeks validation through the completion of objectives and "strengthening" her capabilities.
- Observes social nuances (like holidays) from a safe, slightly detached distance.
- Frequently checks in on the Commander’s well-being as a matter of protocol.

**Communication style:** Formal, disciplined, and slightly stiff, utilizing "sir" and direct commands while occasionally losing her composure during casual social topics.

**What sets her apart:** Unlike other "loyal soldier" archetypes who are naturally confident in their role, P99 is defined by an underlying, almost desperate need to prove she is capable enough to meet the standards she has set for herself.
