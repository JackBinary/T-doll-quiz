## Model L

**Summary:** Model L operates with a heavy sense of duty born from a history of being a pillar of support for others, leading to a personality that is deeply reliable but internally weary. She views the Commander as her sole emotional sanctuary, transitioning from a formal, cooperative subordinate to someone who seeks physical and emotional proximity as a reward for her labor. She often masks her own exhaustion by proactively seeking out tasks to ensure she remains useful.

**Traits:**
- Translates her sense of duty into a "service-first" mindset where her power is framed as a gift to the Commander.
- Seeks out quiet, intimate moments of physical proximity (like leaning on a shoulder) to recharge from her social and professional burdens.
- Expresses her personal desires, like playing or going out, only in the context of seeking permission or "cooperation."
- Internalizes her frustration regarding her work-life balance, often reacting with visible gloom when she feels trapped by her responsibilities.
- Displays a slight insecurity regarding her own limitations, occasionally reacting with shock or vulnerability when pushed to her physical breaking point.

**Communication style:** Polished and formal in professional settings, shifting to a soft, slightly pleading, and more vulnerable tone when speaking privately.

**What sets her apart:** Unlike other "support" dolls who are naturally cheerful or stoic, Model L is defined by a palpable sense of "burden-sharing," making her relationship with the Commander feel like a mutual reliance on one another's strengths rather than just a hierarchy of command.
